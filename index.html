How many of you know the author H. G. Wells?

Invisible Man, Time Machine (he coined the term!), War of the Worlds, First Men in the Moon...

How many of you know he wrote a history book?

He is called father of science fiction. Fairly popular guy. A local, born in Bromley, lived and died in London.

"Outline of History" sold 2 million copies.

You don't need to buy expensive copies for investment.

This book is not hard to find/acquire, considering it was first published ~100 years ago. I bought one for 10 Pounds.

What makes a good book/magazine?

  • Content - engaging stories
  • Print quality - paper weight, typesetting, ink quality and binding

  • Engaging stories in a fun/funny/interesting way.
  • Print quality you can feel.
  • Metrics - pulitzer prize, bestsellers, critical reception...

New books - properties

  • Thin paper not durable
  • Acidic paper has slow burn
  • Glue binding - pages falling off
  • And content up for grabs!
  • Pages easily damaged, you can't fold, you can't dog ear it
  • It degrades on its own
  • Noone knows if a newly printed book is going to be one of the classics, because by definition, does it stand the test of time?

New books - reasons

  • Modern manufacturing
  • Tiktok/instagram era - attention grabbing
Fashion of the moment

Old books

  • Stitched binding
  • Leather covers
  • Heavy paper - original typesetting, sometimes hand-painted pictures
  • Content quality - has it stood the ultimate test (test of time)? Classics are a very good option
  • Compare with the old books
  • Strong binding, you need to force a page to take it out
  • Covers are indestructible
  • Paper you can use as a poster or a postcard

How do you find old books?

  • your friendly local second-hand bookshop
  • eBay
  • charity shops
  • estate sales
